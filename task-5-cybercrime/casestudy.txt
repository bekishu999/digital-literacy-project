UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, especially with the rapid growth of digital payment systems. In this type of fraud, scammers trick users into sharing sensitive banking details such as OTPs or approving fraudulent payment requests.

A typical scenario begins when a fraudster contacts the victim, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer from an online marketplace. The scammer may send a fake link or initiate a “collect request” on a UPI app and claim that the victim needs to approve it to receive money. However, when the victim approves the request, money is actually debited from their account instead of being credited.

Students and individuals who are not fully aware of digital payment systems are often targeted. Once the victim shares an OTP or approves the request, the transaction is completed instantly, making it difficult to reverse. The consequences include financial loss, mental stress, and sometimes loss of trust in digital platforms.

This case highlights the importance of digital awareness and caution. Users must understand that receiving money does not require approval of a payment request. They should avoid sharing OTPs, verify links carefully, and use only trusted applications to ensure their financial safety.
